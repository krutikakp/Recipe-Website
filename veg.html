Vanga chi bhaji

Vanga chi bhaji is a traditional Maharashtrian dish made from eggplant (brinjal/aubergine) cooked with spices and sometimes lentils. It's a flavorful and aromatic vegetarian dish commonly served with rice or Indian bread like chapati or bhakri.

Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a beloved Indian street food dish, especially popular in Maharashtra. It comprises a spicy vegetable mash (bhaji) cooked with a blend of spices and served with soft bread rolls (pav). Typically garnished with butter, onions, and lemon juice, it's known for its rich flavor and is a favorite among food enthusiasts for its delicious and comforting taste.

Delicious Pohe

Kande Pohe is a traditional Maharashtrian breakfast dish made from flattened rice (poha) cooked with onions (kande), spices, and sometimes other ingredients like potatoes, peanuts, and mustard seeds.

Vada Pav

Vada Pav is a popular Indian street food originating from the state of Maharashtra. It consists of a spicy potato fritter (vada) sandwiched between a soft bread roll (pav). Often served with chutneys and fried green chili peppers, it's known for its savory, flavorful taste and affordability, making it a favorite snack for many.

Zanzanit Misal

Misal is a traditional Maharashtrian dish, known for its spicy and flavorful taste. It typically consists of sprouted moth beans cooked with spices, served with a fiery gravy made from onions, tomatoes, and spices. It's often topped with crunchy sev (fried noodles), chopped onions, cilantro, and lemon juice.

Sabudana Khichdi

Sabudana Khichdi is a traditional Indian dish made from soaked tapioca pearls (sago), tempered with spices like cumin seeds, green chilies, and peanuts. It's commonly eaten during fasting periods in India and is cherished for its light, flavorful, and easily digestible nature.

Kothimbir Vadi

Kothimbir vadi is a delicious savory crisp snack from the Maharashtrian cuisine made with gram flour (besan), coriander leaves, peanut, sesame seeds and spices. These savory, herby and flavorful bites can be pan-fried or deep-fried. They are a popular tea-time fritter snack loaded with coriander leaves (cilantro).
